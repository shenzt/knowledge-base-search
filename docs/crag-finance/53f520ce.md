# History of Apple stock splits says investors shouldn't rush in ...

Apple begins trading Monday after a 4-for-1 stock split which could bring in new investors. But the technology giant&#x27;s previous splits have preceded short-term declines in market value.Apple is having a great year, and its 4-for-1 stock split on Monday is expected to make the market's most-valuable company even more attractive to a wider universe of retail investors. But the limited history of Apple stock splits says there is no reason to rush in to buy the lower-priced shares. Two weeks after the previous stock splits, shares of Apple have lost an average of 5.6%, trading negatively in all four instances, according to data from hedge fund trading information platform Kensho. That underperforms the Dow Jones Industrial Average, which tends to be a coin flip in these post-Apple stock split weeks.
